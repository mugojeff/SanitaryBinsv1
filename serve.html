Bedbug & Roaches Solutions

Bed bug infestation is a rising menace in Kenya especially in the urban areas:Nairobi,Nakuru and Mombasa.
Although they do not transmit infectious diseases they pose physical and mental trauma.
Their bites can be very itchy and when scratched can introduce germs into the wounds,some people may also experience mild to severe allergies reactions to the bites.
Since bed bugs usually attack at night they can keep you awake all night depriving you of your sleep causing you irritation exhausting you physically and mentally.
Some people describe their experince with bed bugs as the worst thing that ever happened to them in their lives,when they felt completely drained out and worthless.
Dense urban populations, major movements of people and increasing restrictions on residual insecticides have contributed to a major resurgence in bed bug problems in recent years. Although bedrooms continue to be the site of most infestations, bed bugs have the ability to travel over great distances on clothing, luggage and furniture. This means they are found in a wide range of locations including offices, schools, shops, cinemas and public transport. Controllers should always extend inspections and, if necessary, treatments to other parts of premises found to be infested.Our bed bug & roaches treatment procedure is detailed with a minimum of two sessions per fortnight.

Grain Stores Pest Control & Fumigation

stored gain pest management

WHY?
The effectiveness of a grain store fumigation depends on the two processes that MUST work hand in hand and as a process;
  • Internal fumigation
  • External Pest Control and Fumigation
    • internal fumigation: involves use of fumigant( in general phosphine E.G PHOSTOXIN) this targets the pests inside the sacks, acquired during packaging or on transit to the go down.This should be done on every new consignment to avoid introducing the existing cargo with new pest or can be stored in a separate pile and done after a specific timeline.

    Factors that could affect this process:
    1. Gas used; should be from a licensed PCPB(Pest Control and Produce Board) Supplier to avoid interference with the seal and expiry.Stock just enough to avoid having expired fumigant, once the seal is broken the content should be used completely. see PHOSTOXIN MSDS; https://www.degeschamerica.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/Phostoxin-Tablet-Pellet-manual.pdf
    2. Dose of gas; It is essential to reach a lethal concentration dependent on the quantity of grains to be fumigated. That is a specific number of tablets per metric tonne or tablet per Bushels and vertical storage
    3. Time of exposure; the number of days depend with the external temperatures.For instance for temperature > 20C minimum of 2days nothing less for external temperature < 11C minimum of 14days,
    4. Absence of gas leakage; this interferes with dosage, cleanliness of the warehouse gives no room for potential pest habitat.
    The External Fumigation: involves targeting flying by fogging and crawling insects that have strained from the cargo/cereals. This is to be done without fail after every 3 months. Fogging is a must to target flying insect at the flying stage of insect development.

    • are always looking for hiding places, and once they find a way into your building, it is difficult and costly to get rid of them.
    Customers and clients will not patronize a business with known pest control issues.

    HOW?

  • Proactive Pest Control Management
  • Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

    • Proactive Pest Control Management
    Implementing a proactive pest control program reduces the chances of an infestation escalating out of control and over budget. A successful pest management program depends on a strong partnership between the building’s management, occupants, and a pest control professional.
    Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
    An (IPM) Program is an ecosystem-based strategy that focuses on exclusion, inspection, surveillance, and prevention and not just treatment alone. An IPM program places limits on pest control materials while focusing on proactive prevention and education. Pesticides are used only when needed and must adhere to established guidelines that minimize the risk to either human or environmental concerns. The six major components common to all IPM programs are:
    1. Pest Identification
    2. Assessing pest numbers and the damage done
    3. Guidelines for when management action is needed
    4. Preventing pest problems
    5. Implementing a combination of biological, physical, cultural, and chemical management tools
    6. Monitoring the effect of pest management

    Regulations Compliance

    HersBins is regulated by National Environment Management Authority NEMA/VM/LTW/1987 and Pest Control and Produce Board. No.38784 .We train and retain our staff on the four international guidelines
    1. Precautionary Principle- Waste handlers are required to be prepared and responsible for the protection, preservation and restoration of the environment.
    2. Polluter Pays Principle- All waste producers are legally and financially responsible for safe handling of waste, environmentally sound disposal of waste and creating an incentive to produce less waste.
    3. Duty of Care Principle-stipulates that any person handling or managing substances or related equipment is ethically responsible for applying utmost care.
    4. Proximity Principle- recommends that treatment and disposal of medical and waste take place as near as possible to the point of production as is technically and environmentally possible to minimize risks involved in transport
